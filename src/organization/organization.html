Organization Bylaws

The governing principles and policies that guide our department's operations and activities.

We, the student of the College of Science of University of Rizal System, cognizant of our rights and responsibilities to form us into an organization that shall uphold and embody our ideals, protect our rights, promote our general welfare, foster a more fraternal relationship among ourselves and other, do ordain and promulgate this constitution.

  1. The organization shall be known as TIPA which stands for Technology Innovators through Programming and Algorithm Sciences during its authority and power from the student body, which it represents.
  2. The organization shall have a seal, which shall be truly symbolic and reflective of the organization ideals.
  3. The motto of the organization shall be “Cruising The Path to Boundless Innovation"
  4. The organization shall uphold the ideals and objectives of the college.
  5. The organization shall promote the intellectual, socio-cultural, spiritual and physical well-being of the University of Rizal System students.
  6. The organization shall establish, promote and foster harmonious relationships and better unity among its members and others.
  7. The organization shall develop and enhance the mental ability of its members through assisting and providing them different sources of information.
  1. The objectives of the Technology Innovators through Programming and Algorithm Sciences (TIPA) are as follows:

  1. Develop a more fraternal and harmonious relationship among the students of the Bachelor of Science in Computer Science program.

  2. Develop and enhance the mental ability of its members through the different activities in terms of Information Technology related matters.

  3. Develop the social value of its members through the different activities/programs.

  4. Express different knowledge in computers and share it to others.

  5. To describe the relationship of people to computers and the essential roles of human development, implementation and use of computers.

  6. To provide additional knowledge and information regarding some matters which are not to be tackled or encountered in regular computer lectures/classes.

  7. To help the Alma Mater in the propagation of competitive computer students.

  1. The organization shall recognize that its members are bonafide students of the College of Science who are from Bachelor of Science in Computer Science program.
  2. The payment of PHP 50.00 as membership fee for a school year shall be collected after ​the duly elected officer has been inducted into office by school authority.
  3. The student body must voluntarily agree to the collection of membership fee and must not be forced to pay the membership fee of the organization.
  4. The organization must only collect the membership fee on a face-to-face curriculum for the academic year.
  1. Every student who is member of the organization shall have the rights to:

    1. Express himself and be heard in every meeting.

    2. Participate in all the organization assemblies and other similar activities.

    3. Avail all the benefits extended by the organizations.

    4. The organization must only collect the membership fee on a face-to-face curriculum for the academic year.

  1. It shall be the duty of every member of the organization to:

    1. Honor and respect the College.

    2. Obey the rules and regulations of the College.

    3. Uphold this constitution and the rules emanating from the organization.

  1. Every student who is a member of the organization is obliged to:

    1. Respect and obey the elected officers of the organization.

    2. Participate in whatever activities the organization might hold and share his ideas and suggestions concerning these activities.

The TIPA board of officers shall be elected by the body or may be appointed by the organization adviser, which is composed of the class officers, previous T.I.P.A. officers, and interested students of Bachelor of Science in Computer Science students shall commence after they have been inducted into office and may be re-elected for another term.
  5. No executive committee member shall be an executive officer of another organization
  1. The TIPA board of officers shall perform all tasks of implementing the objectives and principles set forth in this by-law with highest degree of responsibility, efficiency and integrity.
  2. The PRESIDENT shall be the chief officer of the organization. It shall be his/her duty to:

    1. Preside over regular and special meetings of the organization.

    2. Sign all documents and communications of the club.

    3. Present to his/her co-officer together with the adviser the programs of the organization and the guidelines of its policies.

    4. Represent the organization in meetings, which affect the club and its members.

    5. Exercise all those powers that are inherent to his/her position and shall perform such other duties as may be necessary or incident to the performance of his/her function.

    6. Must possess a minimum of one year of prior experience serving in an officer role within the organization.

  3. The VICE-PRESIDENT OF INTERNAL AFFAIRS shall:

    1. Assume the powers and the duties of the President in case of the President’s abuse, incapacity, or request.

    2. Shall be responsible for promoting and formulating activities and initiatives that are academically inclined and directly relevant to the field of computer science.

    3. Assist the President in all matters that he/she may require.

    4. Must possess a minimum of one year of prior experience serving in an officer role within the organization.

  4. The VICE-PRESIDENT OF EXTERNAL AFFAIRS shall:

    1. Assume the powers and the duties of the President in case of the President’s abuse, incapacity, or request.

    2. Shall manage external relations with other organizations within the university, and/or with other entities. They shall be responsible for establishing and maintaining partnerships that propose opportunities for collaboration, thereby fostering excellence among members.

    3. Assist the President in all matters that he/she may require.

    4. Must possess a minimum of one year of prior experience serving in an officer role within the organization.

  5. The GENERAL SECRETARY shall:

    1. Prepare, sign and file all the documents and communication of the organization.

    2. Responsible for the housekeeping of all the records and documents of the organization.

    3. Secure the minutes of the meeting.

    4. Perform such other functions required of the organization and as the President may require.

  6. The ASSOCIATE SECRETARY shall:

    1. Help the Secretary to perform his/her tasks.

    2. Assume the duties of the Secretary in case of his/her absence.

    3. Perform such other functions required of the organization and as the President may require.

  7. The TREASURER shall:

    1. Responsible for the money keeping and all matters concerning funds.

    2. Responsible for all the properties of the organization.

    3. Prepare the budget allocation of the organization.

    4. Perform such other functions required of the organization and as the President may require.

  8. The BUSINESS MANAGER shall:

    1. Oversee and supervise the work of Business Associates, ensuring their tasks align with organizational goals and approved plans. They shall be responsible for identifying, assessing, and managing potential risks, implementing mitigation strategies as needed.

    2. Ensure that all projects are delivered within the approved budget, timeline, and scope.

    3. Escalate issues that cannot be resolved within the team or by Business Associates to the President for timely resolution.

    4. Provide regular status reports on project progress to the President and relevant parties.

    5. Head the Business Committee. In line with this, manage communication between Business Associates, other officers, and stakeholders to ensure smooth operations.

    6. Perform such other functions required of the organization and as the President may require.

  9. The PUBLIC INFORMATION OFFICER shall:

    1. Handle, disseminate, and promote all the information regarding the activities of the organization.

    2. Keep the members of the organization well-informed of its meetings and activities.

    3. Serve as the spokesman of the organization.

    4. Build and maintain a credible image for the organization ;

    5. Perform other duties as may be required by the President.

    6. Head the Publicity Committee.

  10. The BLOCK REPRESENTATIVES shall:

    1. Represent his/her year level in all of the meetings of the organization;

    2. Serve as the grievance desk for their respective year level;

    3. Conceptualize and implement programs and projects, and recommend policies for their respective year level;

    4. Assist in the effective implementation of the organization's programs and projects;

    5. Perform other duties assigned by the President and/or the organization.

  11. The SOCIAL MEDIA MANAGERS shall:

    1. Help the Public Information Officer in all the task and duty given to them;

    2. Shall manage all social media platforms of the organization;

    3. Perform other duties as may be required by the President and/or the organization;

    4. Can be appointed while simultaneously hold a position in the current board of officers.

  12. The EX-OFFICIO shall:

    1. Give guidance and act as consultant to the board of officers when needed;

    2. Assist in the effective implementation of the organization's programs and projects;

    3. Perform other duties assigned by the President and/or the organization;

    4. Must have previously served as an officer.

  13. The BUSINESS ASSOCIATES shall:

    1. Receive clear instructions, access necessary resources, and be informed of project goals, deadlines, and scope.

    2. Maintain communication between the team, Business Manager, and officers; provide status reports; manage risks; and escalate unresolved issues with prior approval from the Business Manager.

    3. Request and gather information, recommend adjustments (with approval), and liaise with the President to ensure projects are delivered within budget, on time, and within scope.

    4. Can be appointed while simultaneously hold a position in the current board of officers.

    5. Perform other functions as required by the President or Business Manager.

  1. There shall be six (6) permanent committees in the TIPA; namely, the Executive Committee, Internal Affairs Committee, Secretariat, Finance Committee, Publicity Committee, and Business Committee. Members of each committee in the TIPA shall be appointed by the President from among the officers of the homeroom organizations upon the recommendation of the chairperson of the committee .
  2. The Executive Committee shall be headed by the President and shall act as the administrative arm of the TIPA.
  3. The Internal Affairs Committee shall be headed by the Vice President and shall take charge of all the matters regarding the members of the TIPA and their functions.
  4. The Secretariat shall be headed by the Secretary and shall be the official TIPA work group
  5. The Finance Committee shall be headed by the Treasurer and shall be assisted by the Auditor and Business Manager. This committee shall take charge of all monetary matters and properties of the TIPA
  6. The Publicity Committee shall be headed by the Public Information Officer and shall be responsible for disseminating information on matters concerning the TIPA.
  7. The Business Committee shall be headed by the Business Manager and shall be responsible for managing, coordinating, and organizing business matters involving TIPA.
  8. Other Committees, permanent or ad hoc in nature, may be created by the TIPA as the need arises .

  2. Membership in the TMS is open to all members of TIPA who have an interest and expertise in technical and media support.
  3. Members of the TMS shall be responsible for:

    1. Providing technical expertise and support for projects and events.

    2. Managing media resources, including audio, visual, and digital media.

    3. Collaborating with other committees and departments to fulfill technical and media needs.

  4. The TMS is contingent upon the following terms and conditions:

    1. The TMS is not authorized to engage in any technical activities outside TIPA without the beneficiary/recipient organization submitting a formal letter addressed to the program head, adviser, and president.

    2. Inclusion of TIPA and the TMS in project proposals and certificates in collaborations is a mandatory requirement.

    3. While the TMS acknowledges that they may not be able to furnish all the necessary equipment utilized for their job, the committee kindly request that consideration be given to providing or lending them the resources they need for a seamless execution.

    4. The TMS do not seek monetary compensation for their technological services; however, the committee requests that provisions be made to ensure the availability of food.

    5. The TMS shall abstain from participating in technological operations for an organization unless the certificates for those who provided technical assistance previously are delivered on or before the date specified by the chairperson.

  2. A monthly report on the receipts a net/or collections made duly signed by the Treasurer and the President, and noted by the TIPA Adviser shall be posted on the TIPA bulletin board and be submitted to the School Head.
  3. The TIPA shaII deposits all funds received and collected shall maintain banking connection for the regular deposit and savings account of the TIPA.
  4. The organization may accept cash donations from any member or any public or private. person or institution, as long as it will not affect or compromise the integrity of the TIPA.
  5. The TIPA may undertake fund-raising activities to subsidize its projects and activities as long as it will not affect or compromise the integrity of the TIPA.
  6. The TIPA shall enjoy the freedom to determine its priorities in the disbursement of its funds, provided that it shall be actually, directly, and exclusively used to support and/or finance TIPA programs, projects and activities.
  7. All disbursement and bank withdrawals shall be made in accordance with the programs, projects and activities of the TIPA embodied in the yearly action plan endorsed by the TIPA Adviser and duly approved by the College Dean, OSDS, and Student Unit Organization Head.
  8. The TIPA President and the Treasurer shall be the signatory of all fund disbursements and bank withdrawals of the TIPA. All fund disbursements and bank withdrawals must be accompanied with a TIPA resolution duly noted by the TIPA Adviser. All expenses must be supported by official receipts. The School Head shall be provided a copy of all the resolutions relative the disbursements made.
  9. The TIPA, through the Treasurer and the Auditor, shall promulgate financial guidelines for proper financial management.
  10. At the end of every activity, the TIPA shall liquidate the expenses before another disbursement. Without this, succeeding bank withdrawals shall not be authorized. flu rids of the organization shall be audited at the end of the term of the TIPA and at the end of every activity.
  11. Financial statements shall be posted in the TIPA Bulletin Board every month, printed in every issue of the School Paper and, the College Dean, College Student Body, and Student Unit Head must be furnished with a copy.
  1. Meeting - Monthly meetings of the TIPA will be held every first and third Friday of the month. Notice and time of the meeting shall be delivered personally, through the consent of the class adviser at least two days before the date set for such a meeting.
  2. Special Meeting - For any purpose unless otherwise provided by statute, a special meeting may be called at any time by the president and/or adviser of the club. The notice of every special meeting shall briefly state the purpose or the agenda of the meeting.
  3. Quorum - A quorum for any meeting of the club shall consist of a simple majority (50%+1) of ​ the members, except as otherwise provided for by the law.
  4. Order of business - The order of annual meeting shall be as follows:

    1. Proof of the required notice of the meeting;

    2. Proof of quorum;

    3. Reading of the minutes of the previous meeting and action taken thereon.

  1. Officers of the TIPA shall be authorized to discipline members who violate any of the provisions of the constitution.
  2. The officers of the organization with the concurrence of the adviser/s shall determine what constitutes disorderly behavior and corresponding disciplinary measure.
  1. Any amendment to or revision of this constitution may be proposed by a member or members of the organization upon a vote of two-thirds (2/3) of all the members of the organization.
  2. Any amendment to or revision of this constitution shall take effect only when ratified by a majority of the votes cast in a plebiscite, which shall be held not later than one month after the approval of such an amendment.
  1. The officers of the TIPA may be impeached on the following grounds :

    1. Culpable violations of the Constitution and By - Laws;

    2. Gross misconduct, violation to person in authority, negligence, and disloyalty to the cause of the TIPA and the school;

    3. Disrespect to positions;

    4. Non - attendance in both regular and special meetings for three (3) times without valid cause; and,

    5. Abuse or misuse of power and authority.

  2. Two-third (2/3) vote of the General Assembly shall be necessary to decide after hearing a case of impeachment.
  3. The decision of the General Assembly shall be final. However, the officer charged shall be informed twenty (20) days prior to his/her impeachment case proceedings, of the charge/charges against him/her , to afford him/her the opportunity to be heard with or without counsel in his/her defense .
  4. Resignation of an elected officer shall be in writing and shall take effect five (5) days after the approval of the majority of the TIPA officers.
  5. Resignation of an appointed offer shall be in writing and shall take effect immediately after the approval of the appointing authority
  6. Any vacancy in any of the positions for the reason of death, resignation, impeachment, shall be filled , subject to the recommendation of the TIPA
  7. Any vacancy in the TIPA, except the position of the President, shall be filled , within ten ( 10 ) school days from the day the position is rendered or considered vacant, by appointment of the President from among the qualified members of the TIPA. Immediately upon appointment, the appointee shall serve the remaining period.
  8. The EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE shall certify as to the vacancy occurring in the TIPA and the TIPA shall certify the fact of appointment of the officer to the vacant position
  1. This constitution shall take effect immediately upon its ratification by the majority of the votes cast in plebiscite called for a purpose and upon approval by the school authorities.
  2. The University Supreme Student Government, the College of Science Student Body, and/or TIPA may propose amendments. Such proposal shall be carried through a resolution duly signed and concurred in by a majority of vote of the University Supreme Student Government, the College of Science Student Body, and/or TIPA officers concerned voting separately .
  3. Proposed amendments shall be transmitted to the office of the College of Science Student Body and University Supreme Student Government at least before holding any conference or events organized for the purpose where the same shall be submitted for consultation, discussion, and deliberation.
  4. Amendments to this Constitution and By-laws shall take effect immediately upon approval of the University Supreme Student Government and College of Science Student Body.
