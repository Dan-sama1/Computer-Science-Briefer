History of Computer Science


From a specialized major within Mathematics to a standalone hub of digital innovation at URS Morong.


The Evolution of Computer Science

The journey of the Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) program is a testament to the university’s adaptability and foresight. Originally, Computer Science was anchored within the Bachelor of Science in Mathematics program, serving as a specialized major. During these foundational years, the curriculum was guided by a dedicated roster of visionary educators, most notably Professor Michael B. Francisco, Professor Lindy A. Alejandro, and Professor Maria Cecilia P. San Jose.

As the global landscape shifted toward digital innovation, these educators recognized that Computer Science needed to evolve into a standalone program. Driven by this vision, Professor Alejandro spearheaded an initiative alongside Professor Francisco and Professor San Jose to establish an independent program under the College of Science.

This dedication came to fruition on March 29, 2021, when the University of Rizal System Board of Regents officially approved and implemented the standalone BSCS program.

TIPA: The Student Voice

In the program's second year, the student community saw the birth of a pioneering organization: Technology Innovators through Programming and Algorithm Sciences (TIPA). Spearheaded by Michelle Pagurayan and the first batch of BSCS students, TIPA was established to be a catalyst for growth and a training ground for future tech leaders.


The Inaugural Commencement

The ultimate measure of success was realized on June 20, 2025. The BS Computer Science program reached its most significant milestone: its inaugural major commencement, producing over 85 highly skilled graduates, including scholars who earned Summa Cum Laude, Magna Cum Laude, and Cum Laude distinctions.



Brief History of the College of Science

The College of Science traces its roots back to the establishment of the Institute of Arts and Sciences in 1984 under MECS Order No. 16, s. 1984. From 1984 to 2005, the Institute nurtured scholars in Biology, Psychology, Mathematics, and Guidance and Counseling. In 2005, it was formally renamed the College of Science.
